Osmosis = water follows particles

Roots are tiny water gates. If outside solution is too strong, water leaves cells; too weak, cells overfill. The sweet spot keeps leaves perky and nutrients flowing.

How plants cope

  • Open/close water channels (aquaporins)
  • Stash small molecules (osmolytes) to hold water
  • Route flow through cell walls vs. through cells

Glossary

Colligative properties
Water behavior changes caused simply by how many particles are dissolved (not which kind).
Osmotic pressure
The pressure needed to stop water from moving toward a stronger solution across a membrane.
Vapor pressure lowering
Dissolved particles make it harder for water to evaporate.
Boiling point elevation
More particles ‚Üí water boils at a slightly higher temperature.
Freezing point depression
More particles ‚Üí water freezes at a lower temperature.
