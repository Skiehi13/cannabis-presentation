Osmosis = water follows particles

Roots are tiny water gates. If outside solution is too strong, water leaves cells; too weak, cells overfill. The sweet spot keeps leaves perky and nutrients flowing.

How plants cope

  • Open/close water channels (aquaporins)
  • Stash small molecules (osmolytes) to hold water
  • Route flow through cell walls vs. through cells

Hydroponics = managing solution strength

  • EC tracks ion strength → a handy proxy for osmotic pressure.
  • Too low EC → underfeeding, slow growth. Too high → osmotic stress, tip burn.
  • Typical ranges: 0.8–1.3 seedling • 1.2–1.6 veg • 1.6–2.0 flower.
HAPPY GROWING!

pH quick hits for cultivators

  • pH sets what nutrients are available; off-range pH can “lock out” key ions.
  • Targets (typical): hydro 5.5–6.5 • coco 5.8–6.3 • soil 6.2–7.0.
  • Adjust slowly; log changes and re-check after mixing.
  • Symptoms: high pH → Fe/Mn lockout; low pH → Ca/Mg issues.
  • Measure well: calibrate, mind temp, rinse between readings.

Reaction Coordinates

  • Idea: Imagine a hill between “before” and “after.” The path over it is the reaction coordinate.
  • Hump: Highest point = transition state. Hill height = activation energy (Ea).
  • Why growers care: THCA → THC + CO₂ needs heat to clear the hump—too low = slow; too high = terpene loss.
HAPPY GROWING!

THCA → THC + CO₂ (Decarboxylation)

  • Baseline Ea: ~120 kJ·mol⁻¹
  • Practical window: ~110–130 °C balances conversion vs. terpenes
  • Environment helps: oil/solvent matrix & even heating can effectively lower the barrier

Grower cues

  • Gentle ramp to temp; avoid hot spots
  • Closed/inert setups limit oxidation
  • Measure cannabinoids/terpenes to tune

Glossary

Colligative properties
Water behavior changes caused simply by how many particles are dissolved (not which kind).
Osmotic pressure
The pressure needed to stop water from moving toward a stronger solution across a membrane.
Vapor pressure lowering
Dissolved particles make it harder for water to evaporate.
Boiling point elevation
More particles → water boils at a slightly higher temperature.
Freezing point depression
More particles → water freezes at a slightly lower temperature.
