Osmosis = water follows particles

Roots are tiny water gates. If outside solution is too strong, water leaves cells; too weak, cells overfill. The sweet spot keeps leaves perky and nutrients flowing.

How plants cope

  • Open/close water channels (aquaporins)
  • Stash small molecules (osmolytes) to hold water
  • Route flow through cell walls vs. through cells
HAPPY GROWING!

Hydroponics = managing solution strength

  • EC tracks ion strength → a handy proxy for osmotic pressure.
  • Too low EC → underfeeding, slow growth. Too high → osmotic stress, tip burn.
  • Typical ranges: 0.8–1.3 seedling • 1.2–1.6 veg • 1.6–2.0 flower.

pH quick hits for cultivators

  • pH sets what nutrients are available; off-range pH can “lock out” key ions.
  • Targets (typical): hydro 5.5–6.5 • coco 5.8–6.3 • soil 6.2–7.0.
  • Adjust slowly; log changes and re-check after mixing.
  • Symptoms: high pH → Fe/Mn lockout; low pH → Ca/Mg issues.
  • Measure well: calibrate, mind temp, rinse between readings.

Kinetics — Model & External Factors

Reaction Coordinate & Activation Energy

  • Transition state sits at the energy maximum; height is activation energy.
  • Temperature raises molecular energy; more molecules surpass the barrier (Arrhenius).
  • Catalysts lower effective barrier without changing ΔG.

Applied to Cannabis

  • THCA → THC + CO₂ requires heat; too low = slow conversion, too high = terpene loss.
  • Practical window ~110–130 °C balances conversion and aroma preservation.
  • Even heating and appropriate matrices (oil/solvent) improve outcomes.

Glossary

Colligative properties
Water behavior changes caused by how many particles are dissolved—not which kind.
Vapor pressure lowering
Dissolved particles make it harder for water to evaporate.
Boiling point elevation
More particles → water boils at a slightly higher temperature.
Freezing point depression
More particles → water freezes at a lower temperature than 0 °C.
Osmotic pressure
Pressure needed to stop water from moving toward a stronger solution across a membrane.
Solutes
Stuff dissolved in a liquid (like salts or sugars). More solute = bigger colligative effects.
Acidic
pH < 7. Tends to donate H⁺. Think lemon juice, cola.
Alkaline (basic)
pH > 7. Tends to accept H⁺. Think baking soda, soap.
pH scale / meter
0–14 scale of acidity/alkalinity. pH meters measure hydrogen ion activity—calibrate them!
Kinetics
How fast reactions happen (rates), not whether they’re favorable (that’s thermodynamics).
Photosynthesis
Plants turn light + CO₂ + water into sugars. Your buds say thank you.
Respiration
Plants burn sugars for energy—day and night business.
Nutrient uptake
Roots absorbing ions from solution—depends on pH, EC, and membranes behaving.
Reaction coordinate
The “path” over an energy hill from reactants to products. Peak = transition state.
Activation energy
The push needed to climb the hill. Lower Ea → faster reaction.
Decarboxylation
Heat-driven loss of CO₂. THCA → THC + CO₂ during decarb.
Terpenes
Volatile aroma molecules—tasty but heat-sensitive.
Catalyst
Helps the reaction by lowering the barrier (Ea) without being consumed.
THC (−)-Δ⁹-trans-Tetrahydrocannabinol
Psychoactive cannabinoid formed by decarboxylating THCA.
CBD (−)-trans-Cannabidiol
Non-intoxicating cannabinoid with a different ring connectivity than THC.
